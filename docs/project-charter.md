# Project Charter  
**Sales Performance Optimization Initiative**  
*Fortune 100 Insurance Company (Anonymized)*

---

## Project Overview
This initiative focused on improving sales performance visibility and strengthening feedback loops between operational changes and frontline outcomes within a high-volume inbound insurance sales environment. The project aimed to provide leadership with clearer insights into performance drivers and enable faster, data-informed decision-making.

All content is anonymized and reconstructed to demonstrate project leadership and delivery approach without exposing proprietary information.

---

## Business Problem
Sales performance fluctuated following operational and strategic changes, with limited visibility into which actions were driving results. Leadership lacked a consistent KPI framework and structured reporting cadence to quickly assess impact and adjust strategies.

---

## Project Objectives
- Establish a standardized KPI framework for sales performance
- Improve clarity around performance trends after operational changes
- Enable faster feedback loops between data, leadership, and execution
- Provide structured documentation to support future initiatives

---

## Success Criteria
The project would be considered successful if:
- KPIs are clearly defined, understood, and consistently tracked
- Leadership can identify performance trends within defined reporting cycles
- Operational adjustments can be evaluated more quickly after implementation
- Documentation enables reuse of the framework for similar initiatives

---

## Scope Definition

### In Scope
- KPI definition and alignment
- Performance analysis and reporting
- Stakeholder communication and status updates
- Risk identification and mitigation
- Documentation and handoff

### Out of Scope
- Compensation or incentive structure changes
- Policy, pricing, or underwriting changes
- Engineering or system development
- Headcount or staffing model changes

---

## Key Stakeholders
- Frontline sales agents
- Sales team leadership
- Sales operations management
- Analytics and reporting partners

---

## Timeline & Milestones (8–10 Weeks)

**Week 1–2: Discovery & Baseline**
- Define project goals and constraints
- Establish baseline KPIs
- Identify key stakeholders
- Confirm reporting cadence

**Week 3–4: Framework Design**
- Finalize KPI definitions and measurement approach
- Design reporting structure
- Identify risks and dependencies

**Week 5–6: Execution & Monitoring**
- Track performance following operational changes
- Produce structured status reports
- Identify emerging trends and issues

**Week 7–8: Optimization**
- Refine metrics and reporting based on feedback
- Surface insights and recommendations
- Document decisions and tradeoffs

**Week 9–10: Handoff & Closeout**
- Final reporting and documentation
- Retrospective and lessons learned
- Transition framework for ongoing use

## Risks & Assumptions

### Key Risks
- Data lag impacting timely analysis
- Resistance to change from frontline teams
- Misalignment on KPI interpretation
- Limited control over upstream policy changes

### Assumptions
- Data sources remain available and reliable
- Stakeholders engage in regular feedback cycles
- Operational changes continue during the project window

---

## Constraints
- No access to modify core systems
- Limited ability to influence policy-level decisions
- Project executed alongside ongoing operational responsibilities
