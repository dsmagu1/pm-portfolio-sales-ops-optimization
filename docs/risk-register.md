# Risk Register  
**Sales Performance Optimization Initiative**

---

## Purpose
This document identifies key risks that could impact delivery, data accuracy, stakeholder alignment, or outcome interpretation during the project lifecycle. Risks were tracked continuously and reviewed as part of regular status updates.

---

## Risk Assessment Approach
Risks were evaluated based on:
- Likelihood of occurrence
- Potential impact on project objectives
- Ability to mitigate or influence the outcome

Each risk includes a mitigation strategy and monitoring plan.

---

## Identified Risks

### Risk 1: Data Lag or Reporting Delays
**Description:**  
Delays in data availability could limit the ability to assess the immediate impact of operational changes.

**Impact:**  
Medium

**Likelihood:**  
High

**Mitigation Strategy:**  
- Focus analysis on trend direction rather than real-time results  
- Set expectations with stakeholders regarding reporting windows  
- Avoid premature conclusions based on partial data

**Owner:**  
Project Lead

---

### Risk 2: Misinterpretation of KPIs
**Description:**  
Stakeholders may interpret metrics differently or overreact to short-term fluctuations.

**Impact:**  
High

**Likelihood:**  
Medium

**Mitigation Strategy:**  
- Clearly document KPI definitions and usage guidelines  
- Review metrics in combination rather than isolation  
- Reinforce trend-based interpretation during updates

**Owner:**  
Project Lead

---

### Risk 3: Resistance to Operational Changes
**Description:**  
Frontline teams may resist or inconsistently adopt changes, affecting performance outcomes.

**Impact:**  
Medium

**Likelihood:**  
Medium

**Mitigation Strategy:**  
- Communicate intent and rationale behind changes  
- Monitor adoption indicators where possible  
- Incorporate feedback into iterative adjustments

**Owner:**  
Sales Leadership / Operations

---

### Risk 4: External Policy or Market Changes
**Description:**  
Policy, pricing, or regulatory changes outside project control could impact performance metrics.

**Impact:**  
High

**Likelihood:**  
Low

**Mitigation Strategy:**  
- Document external changes alongside performance trends  
- Avoid attributing outcomes solely to internal initiatives  
- Adjust analysis to account for external influences

**Owner:**  
Operations Management

---

### Risk 5: Limited Authority or Control
**Description:**  
Project execution occurs without direct authority over systems, staffing, or policy decisions.

**Impact:**  
Medium

**Likelihood:**  
High

**Mitigation Strategy:**  
- Focus scope on analysis, visibility, and recommendations  
- Maintain strong stakeholder alignment  
- Clearly document constraints and dependencies

**Owner:**  
Project Lead

---

### Risk 6: Stakeholder Availability
**Description:**  
Competing priorities may limit stakeholder engagement or feedback.

**Impact:**  
Medium

**Likelihood:**  
Medium

**Mitigation Strategy:**  
- Establish regular communication cadence  
- Keep updates concise and action-oriented  
- Capture decisions and follow-ups asynchronously when needed

**Owner:**  
Project Lead

---

## Monitoring & Review
- Risks reviewed during regular status updates  
- New risks logged as identified  
- Mitigation strategies adjusted based on evolving conditions

---

## Outcome
Proactive risk identification and mitigation supported smoother execution, clearer communication, and more informed decision-making throughout the project lifecycle.
